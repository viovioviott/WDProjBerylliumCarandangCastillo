Instructions:

Once the user has clicked “Play” on the main menu, and has entered the actual simulation, the sky of the epoch January 1, 2000 at 12:00 AM (UTC) in the location Latitude: 0, Longitude: 0 will be displayed. From there you may input in the top left corner of the software what date, time, and location you would like to simulate.

To move the camera, you can use the W and S keys to move the camera up and down. You may use the A and D keys to move it left or right respectively, and you may use the up and down arrow keys to zoom in and zoom out respectively as well. You may hover over the objects in the sky to reveal information about them such as their orbital elements, their distance from Earth, parallax, magnitude, alt-az coordinates, etc.

On the bottom left are the options for adjusting the atmospheric conditions, toggling the guides/lines as well as the actual constellation lines/boundaries.

On the top right of the screen, you may type in the search bar to look for certain celestial bodies, and to the right of that still is the settings button where one can adjust the camera/telescope settings, as well as find view the credits that the developers used in making the app.
